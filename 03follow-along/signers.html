Who were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers include

Thomas Jefferson
Jefferson was born April 13, 1743, on his father’s plantation of Shadwell located along the Rivanna River near the Blue Ridge Mountains of colonial Virginia.1 His father Peter Jefferson was a successful planter and surveyor, and his mother, Jane Randolph, was a member of one of Virginia’s most distinguished families. When Jefferson was fourteen, his father died, and he inherited a sizeable estate of approximately 5,000 acres. That inheritance included the house at Shadwell, but Jefferson dreamed of living on a mountain
John Adams
John Adams (October 30, 1735 – July 4, 1826) was an American Founding Father who served as the second president of the United States from 1797 to 1801. Before his presidency, he was a leader of the American Revolution that achieved independence from Great Britain.
