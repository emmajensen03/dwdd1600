Natural Nails

Natural vs Artificial

Natural nails and artificial nails each have their own advantages and appeal, depending on personal preference and lifestyle. Natural nails, made of keratin, can be strong and healthy with proper care, including regular trimming, moisturizing, and avoiding harsh chemicals. They offer a more natural look and feel but may be prone to breakage if not maintained properly. On the other hand, artificial nails, such as acrylics, gels, and dip powders, provide durability and aesthetic versatility, allowing for longer lengths and intricate designs. However, they require professional application and maintenance, and prolonged use can weaken the natural nails underneath. Ultimately, the choice between natural and artificial nails depends on an individual’s priorities—whether they value low-maintenance, natural beauty, or long-lasting, customizable styles.

While artificial nails offer convenience and style, it’s important to give natural nails time to breathe between applications to prevent damage. Regular hydration, a balanced diet, and proper nail care routines can help maintain nail health, whether you choose to keep them natural or enhance them with artificial options. Finding a balance between beauty and nail care ensures strong, beautiful nails in the long run.

What is Soft Gel

  1. Flexible Gel-Based Nail Enhancement – Soft gel is a lightweight, flexible nail coating made from a gel formula that is cured under a UV or LED lamp, providing a more natural feel compared to hard gel or acrylics.
  2. Soak-Off Formula – Unlike hard gel, soft gel can be easily removed by soaking in acetone, making it a gentler option that reduces damage to the natural nails.
  3. Used for Overlays and Extensions – Soft gel is commonly used as an overlay to strengthen natural nails or as full-coverage gel tips for extensions, offering durability while maintaining a thinner, more natural look.
